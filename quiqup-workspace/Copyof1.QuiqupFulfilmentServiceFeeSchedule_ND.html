Invoicing and Payment terms

VAT

All rates are exclusive of 5% VAT

Invoicing

Invoices will be issued on a monthly basis

Invoices are generated by the 10th of each month.

Payment terms

Payment is due within 10 calendar days of invoice issue date.

Payment method

Automatic Card payment
The registered card on your account will be automatically charged, and payment will be processed via card payment. 
If payment fails, we will retry daily for 30 days. 
After the second attempt of charging, if payment fails, service will be suspended until payment is received.

COD reconciliation

COD remittance will take place on a monthly basis. Along with your invoice, you will also receive a Delivery report and a COD report (detailing the cash collected).

COD payments are released after your written confirmation has been received.

Once we receive your written confirmation on the cash collected, we will:

  • Perform a net-off between your invoice and the COD amount collected.
  • Settle any balance if the COD collected exceeds the invoice value.

As we process payments twice weekly, your payment will be included in the soonest cycle after your confirmation is received.
In the event of any delay in settling the invoice, Quiqup reserves the right to hold COD transfers until the payment is settled.

Quarterly Price Review

A ramp-up period of three (3) months from the Effective Date ("Ramp-Up Period") shall be provided to the Customer to reach the Expected Volume. During the Ramp-Up Period, pricing shall remain fixed as per the initial rate.

At the end of each calendar quarter, the actual volume of services used by the Customer will be reviewed. Based on the actual volume relative to the Expected Volume, pricing shall be revised accordingly:

If actual volumes are equal to or greater than the Expected Volume, the current pricing will remain in effect.

If actual volumes are below the Expected Volume (after the Ramp-Up Period), Quiqup reserves the right to adjust the pricing upward to reflect the lower volume.
